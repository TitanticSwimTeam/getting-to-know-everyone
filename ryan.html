Greatest Innovations

  • The Internet. Is this one even a question? The internet and the world wide web have fundamentally changed the way societies operate, and we're still feeling and managing the side-effects of having such levels of communication in our lives.
  • Moore's Law. Maybe it's not the innvoation itself, but the dramatic growth in computing power and the shrinking volume that is required to contain that power is not something to scoff at or dismiss.
  • Public Key Encryption. Okay so maybe this isn't the "greatest" computing innovation of all time, but as someone who is very interested in cryptography, the notion of assymmetric encryption is so influential in how we transfer data over the internet today. TLS, Certificates, SSH, etc. are all dependent on this concept.
